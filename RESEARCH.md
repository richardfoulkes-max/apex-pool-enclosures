# Pool Heater/Chiller Acoustic Enclosure - Market Research

## Project Context
User has semi-sunken pump enclosure (already quiet). The **heater/chiller** is the noise source.
Key challenge: Soundproofing WITH ventilation (heat dissipation required).

### Key Technical Specs (Industry Standard)

**Noise Reduction:**
- Entry level: 10-14 dB (noticeable improvement)
- Mid-range: 15-18 dB (significant reduction)
- Premium: 20-25 dB (near-silent)
- Note: 10 dB = perceived halving of sound

**Panel Construction (typical):**
- 50mm thick acoustic panels (100mm for premium)
- Outer: 1.2mm galvanized steel
- Inner: 0.8mm perforated steel
- Fill: Mineral fibre 48kg/m³ density

**Ventilation Solutions:**
- Acoustic louvres (NoiseTrap Blox style)
- Labyrinth air paths
- Sound-absorbing baffles at intake/exhaust

---

## Design Challenges

### The Ventilation Paradox
- Sound travels through air → openings leak sound
- Heat pumps need airflow → can't fully seal
- Solution: Labyrinth paths, acoustic louvres, baffled vents

### Low Frequency Problem
- Heat pumps produce problematic low-frequency noise (compressor hum)
- Traditional barriers less effective at low frequencies
- May need mass + absorption combination

### Heat Dissipation
- Heaters/chillers generate significant heat
- Enclosure must not trap heat (equipment damage)
- Airflow path critical to design
