APE-HC1 - Acoustic Heater/Chiller Enclosure

With Integrated Acoustic Chimney for Top-Exhaust Heat Pumps
Model: APE-HC1 v2.0 | Rev 2.0 | December 2025

1. Product Overview

The APE-HC1 is an acoustic enclosure designed specifically for top-exhaust pool heat pumps. It features an integrated acoustic chimney that attenuates noise from the vertical exhaust fans while maintaining full airflow capacity. Target noise reduction: 15-20 dB(A).

3. Airflow Design

Top-Exhaust Chimney Design

Unlike side-to-side airflow enclosures, this design accommodates heat pumps with vertical exhaust fans. Air enters through louvres on three sides, passes through the heat pump, and exits vertically through the acoustic chimney.

5.1 Airflow Requirements

Heat Pump Capacity 44 kW Heating mode

5.2 Provided Louvre Area

Location Gross Area @ 50% Free Area
Front panel (intake) 1400 × 700 = 0.98 m² 0.49 m²
Left panel (intake) 900 × 700 = 0.63 m² 0.32 m²
Right panel (intake) 900 × 700 = 0.63 m² 0.32 m²
Total Intake 2.24 m² 1.12 m²
Chimney top (exhaust) 1200 × 700 = 0.84 m² 0.42 m²
Airflow Adequacy: Effective intake area (1.12 m²) at 2.0 m/s velocity provides ~8,000 m³/h. For full 11,000 m³/h, face velocity increases to ~2.7 m/s, which remains acceptable with slight acoustic penalty.

5.3 Temperature Rise

With proper airflow maintained:

Heat input to enclosure ~5 kW (compressor + fans + solar)
Air mass flow rate 3.7 kg/s @ 11,000 m³/h
Temperature rise ~1.4°C
Thermal Performance: The enclosure will NOT overheat the heat pump. With maintained airflow, temperature rise is only 1-2°C above ambient.

7. Installation Requirements

7.1 Site Requirements

Requirement Specification
Foundation Level concrete pad, 150mm thick, 1600 × 1100mm minimum
Clearance - Front 1000mm minimum for door opening and service
Clearance - Sides 500mm minimum for airflow intake
Clearance - Rear 400mm for pipe connections
Clearance - Above Unobstructed (vertical exhaust)
Drainage Floor drain connection to site drainage
Critical: Do not position enclosure under overhangs, trees, or structures that would obstruct the vertical exhaust from the chimney. The top must have clear access to open air.

7.2 Equipment Placement

  • Position heat pump centrally within enclosure
  • Ensure fan outlets align with chimney opening
  • Maintain minimum 100mm clearance around equipment
  • Connect water pipes through rear panel grommets
  • Route electrical through floor cable glands
  • Ensure condensate drain is connected before closing
