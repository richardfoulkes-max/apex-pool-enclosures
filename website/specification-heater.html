1. Product Overview

The APE-HC series is an acoustic enclosure designed to reduce noise from pool heat pumps and chillers while maintaining adequate ventilation for equipment operation. The enclosure uses a combination of acoustic panels and louvred ventilation to achieve up to 18dB noise reduction.

3. Acoustic Design

The Ventilation Paradox

Heat pumps require continuous airflow but sound travels through air openings. The APE-HC solves this with:

  • Acoustic Louvres: Sound-attenuating air passages that block direct sound paths
  • Labyrinth Path: Sound must travel around multiple bends, losing energy at each turn
  • Absorptive Lining: Mineral wool panels absorb sound inside the enclosure
  • Mass Barrier: 2mm aluminium walls block airborne sound transmission

4. Ventilation System

4.1 Airflow Requirements

Heat pumps require adequate airflow to operate efficiently. Restricting airflow causes:

  • Reduced heating/cooling capacity
  • Increased energy consumption
  • Compressor overheating and shutdown
  • Premature equipment failure

7. Installation Requirements

7.1 Site Requirements

Requirement Specification
Foundation Level concrete pad, 100mm thick minimum
Clearance - Front 1000mm minimum for door opening and service
Clearance - Sides 300mm minimum (500mm preferred for airflow)
Clearance - Rear 300mm for pipe connections
Drainage Floor drain connection to site drainage

7.2 Equipment Placement

  • Position heat pump with fan discharge facing exhaust louvre side
  • Maintain minimum 150mm clearance around equipment
  • Connect water pipes through rear panel grommets
  • Route electrical through floor cable glands
  • Ensure condensate drain is connected before closing
Critical: Do not obstruct intake or exhaust louvres. Blocked airflow will cause heat pump malfunction and void equipment warranty.
